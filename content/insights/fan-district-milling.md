Paving in The Fan District of Richmond, Virginia presents a unique set of engineering challenges. With century-old brick foundations, extremely tight alleyways, and strict municipal noise ordinances, a standard paving operation is guaranteed to fail. 

Recently, J. Worden & Sons Paving was contracted to execute a massive 50,000 square foot structural milling and overlay operation for a commercial property in the heart of The Fan.

## The Engineering Challenge

The existing asphalt had suffered from severe *alligator cracking* and subgrade failure due to years of improper water drainage and heavy delivery truck traffic. Patching was not an option. The lot required a 2-inch deep profile mill to remove the failing surface course without disturbing the historic cobblestone sub-base hidden beneath.

### Logistical Constraints:
1. **Zero-Tolerance Clearances:** Our heavy Wirtgen milling machines had to navigate clearances with less than 6 inches of tolerance against historic brick walls.
2. **Overnight Execution:** To prevent disruption to local businesses, the entire operation—milling, sweeping, tack coating, and paving—had to be completed between 9:00 PM and 6:00 AM.
3. **Vibratory Impact:** We had to utilize static rolling and low-amplitude vibratory compaction to achieve maximum asphalt density without causing structural vibrations that could damage adjacent 19th-century foundations.

## Execution and Materials

We deployed a fleet of three tri-axle dump trucks, a Wirtgen W 120 Fi compact milling machine, and a multi-ton HAMM tandem roller.

We executed the mill perfectly, sweeping the surface to a completely clean canvas. We then applied a heavy coat of CSS-1H tack emulsion to ensure a monolithic bond between the base and the new surface course. 

For the surface, we elected to use **VDOT SM-9.5A Hot Mix Asphalt**, engineered specifically for high-durability parking surfaces with excellent water shedding properties. 

## The Result

By 5:30 AM, our heavy iron was off the lot, and the property owner was left with a perfectly graded, structurally sound, and beautifully black commercial parking facility.
